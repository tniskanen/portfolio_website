About

I'm passionate about using data to understand the world more clearly. From building machine learning pipelines to exploring patterns in real-world datasets, I'm driven by the intersection of curiosity and analysis.

Background

Childhood

Growing up in North Conway, New Hampshire before moving to Klamath Falls, Oregon, I spent most of my childhood developing a passion for hockey that would eventually take me to Washington and Montana, competing on traveling teams with aspirations of playing at the collegiate level. Hockey shaped the way I think about effort and outcomes. It taught me that hard work alone is not enough, that real results come from sustained focus applied over time, and that the people you surround yourself with matter just as much as the work itself.

College

When the pandemic brought that chapter to a close, I enrolled at Arizona State University as a neuroscience major, drawn to understanding how the brain works. Over time, I found myself filling my elective schedule with data science courses and enjoying them more than anything else I was studying. The combination of technical rigor, software, and logic-driven decision making resonated with me in a way that felt both practical and intellectually engaging, and I made the switch to a full data science major from there.

Next Steps

Now finishing my degree, I find myself eager to move beyond the classroom. College has given me a wide view of how expansive the field of data science really is, and with that comes a clear sense of how much more there is to learn by actually doing the work. I am ready to bring the focus and discipline I developed as an athlete into a professional environment where the problems are real, the stakes matter, and the learning never stops.
